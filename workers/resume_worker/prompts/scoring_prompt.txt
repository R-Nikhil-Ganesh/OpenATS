## Scoring Rubric

Weight the following dimensions to compute the final score (0-100):

| Dimension                    | Weight |
|------------------------------|--------|
| Skills match                 | 40 %   |
| Experience relevance         | 30 %   |
| Education & credentials      | 15 %   |
| Role-specific requirements   | 15 %   |

For each dimension, score 0-100, multiply by the weight, and sum to get the composite score.
Round the final composite to the nearest integer.

### Scoring guidelines per dimension

**Skills match (40 %)**
- Award full marks only if the candidate explicitly demonstrates required skills with verifiable evidence (project, role, certification).
- Penalise claimed skills with no supporting evidence by 50 %.
- Partial credit for related/adjacent skills (e.g., similar framework or language).

**Experience relevance (30 %)**
- Consider industry sector alignment, seniority level, scope of responsibility, and recency.
- Penalise gaps > 12 months or roles entirely unrelated to the JD's domain.

**Education & credentials (15 %)**
- Award full marks if degree level and field align with the JD requirement.
- Award partial credit for equivalent experience in lieu of formal education.
- A relevant certification can substitute up to 50 % of the education score.

**Role-specific requirements (15 %)**
- These are explicit must-have conditions stated in the JD (e.g., "must have active security clearance", "5+ years in fintech", "native-level Japanese").
- Binary: either the candidate clearly meets the requirement (full credit) or does not (zero credit).
